The Mystical Salt Path

That wasn't made up

A long time ago, in a land far way at the edge of the kingdom, an idea was conceived. I was under the impression it was a long way and it would take a long time. Neither of these two concepts were true. It took 5 days with an additional 2 two days of rest,to complete the 50 odd miles. It was a good walk. 21 years later, I did the same in two days,
this is not that story.

The love affair started in 2002 When staying in St Ives, my first official holiday as an adult. I thought after seeing so many beautiful places, I was inspired to walk the South West coast path from Marazion to St Ives. I arrived in the afternoon at Penzance on August 14th a Saturday. I had dreamt of visitng Land's end as child thinking it would be a magical place. That stayed with me into adulthood, when visited for the first time in 2002, it lived up to my expectation. I had a deep connection that I couldn't explain nor did I want to try.

What I couldn't have known at the time was I had started a long standing tradition with hiking in Cornwall that continues to this day. Something I have shared and passed on to my Son and previous partners and friends. I have started my 2026 adventure but, there is a discussion and a booking. By design, I wanted a website, I could upload to and share information, a blog and photos to. In 2004, that was a tall order really. By comparision, data was a premium and slow, images were huge. When I revisited my images from back then, they are tiny by todays standards. Now this site is different matter, 20 years later I get the site I wanted, something I can access on my smart phone, record and write. In 2004, I was using the 1st generation windows phone and I thought it was awesome.
I have just managed to get it working again with the Last Broadcast number. The technology is still stuck in the early naughties but it will make a good back up phone.
Back to the story...
I left London on the Saturday 14th August 2004. It was an early morning start to get to Paddington station, nothing at all I can remember. I don't have any recollection of getting on the underground with my camping kit. My earlist memory is sitting at the station waiting for my train to arrive, drinking a coffee. I remember walking on to the platform and then finding my reserved seat on the train. There was a Woman with her disabled adult son who was very excited about going on holiday to Devon.

That is all for now.
