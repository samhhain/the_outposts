Greetings from the end of the kingdom 2026

Part One

21/07/2026

The adventure started on a high, we left at 7am on the dot, well it was 6:59 but I hovered so that we could leave on time. The car rolled off the drive and the warning light fo the tyres came on and told me to inflate them. The one thing I forgot to do the night before. The first garage we came to had a machine that was out of order, I drove to the to next and as I grabbed my wallet I split my thumb nail on the door. I took these as bad omens... but not really. Finally, and only after 15 minutes we were on the M25. It was pretty much plain sailing and then onto the M3, we stopped at Fleet for a break and some breakfast, Lucas was starving. We got back on the road and didn't hit any traffic until Devon???I should point out that this was the first day of summer holiday and a lot of the population head west on this day, including us.

Another stop at Exeter services for stretch and a drink and then on with only an hour or so to go. Finding the campsite wasn't that difficult, it was at the bottom of a village on a large hill in a farmers field but to our horror, it was extremely basic. There was one portaloo for women and one for men, that's it, no showers, no washing up station and a tap for running water. If you turned up with a campervan, with all of your own facilities, this would be perfect. The scenery was beautiful and the field well kept. The pitches were flat and grass low, it was clear it was and is well looked after, it was just not for us. Since I had paid for 3 nights, not a lot I might add and I realise why now, I sat in the car with good(ish) phone reception and found us Another campsite on 'pitchup'. It was 20 minutes away and had showers and looked clean from the photos. When we arrived I nearly crashed into the entrance as it was on a steep hill and at a 45 degree turn, so we slid down luckily didn't hit anything. We had pitch 4 not too bid and probably a little too small for our tent but we made do. It is a cute site with an amazing view of a the valley it sits on and a small view of the sea to the right. The showers were clean and warm and it had a washing area and two fire pits. The only problem we were situated in between a group of young people/ adults who were in full blown party mode and England were playing that night. We are used to a site with a noise curfew, well I am, it doesn't seem to bother Lucas.
